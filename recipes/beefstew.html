Beefstew, the Flemish way



Story

Beefstew might be the pinnacle of Flemish cuisine.
Historically seen as a way to make mediocre meat delicious. However, today it is seen as a luxury dish.
This is most likely due to the time it takes to prepare having become a more valuable comodity.

If you follow this recipe you will end up with an amazing and tasty take on stew.
Although one should consider this to take around two to three hours.


Ingredients for 4 persons

  • 1.5kg Beef - Preferably chuck roast, cut in cubes
    • T
  • Beer: Two bottles of dark belgian beer to your liking
  • Bouquet Garni: Bind together the following fresh spices:
    • 3 sticks of Thyme
    • 2 Bay leaves
    • 3 sticks of Parsley
  • 2 large onions
  • 2 Slices of dark wholewheat bread
  • 2 tablespoons of mustard
  • 2 tablespoons of applesyrup
  • 1 cup of flour
  • 50 grams of butter

Ingredients for 4 persons

  1. Take the beef and roll it through the flour
  2. Use a preheated castiron skillet to stirfry the beef in about half of the butter, it needs to be browned on the outside.
    This is to prevent moisture from leaving the meat during the rest of the process, it can remain rare on the inside.
    Make sure to not fry too much meat at the same time.
  3. Remove the beef from the skillet
  4. Dice the onions
  5. Add the remained of the butter to the skillet, add the onions, bring to a simmer
